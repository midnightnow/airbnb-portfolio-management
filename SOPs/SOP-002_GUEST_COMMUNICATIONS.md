# SOP-002: Guest Communications & Support
## Managing Guest Interactions from Inquiry to Review

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-11  
**Owner:** Virtual Assistant (VA) / Agency Manager  
**Purpose:** Standardized protocols for all guest communications to ensure 5-star experiences and operational efficiency

---

## Overview

Guest communication is the backbone of your 4.9+ star rating and repeat booking rate. This SOP covers every guest interaction from initial inquiry through post-stay review.

**Core Principles:**
1. **Speed:** Respond within 30 minutes during business hours (8 AM - 10 PM local time)
2. **Professionalism:** Friendly but polished, like a boutique hotel
3. **Proactivity:** Anticipate needs before guests ask
4. **Problem-Solving:** Own issues, fix fast, over-communicate
5. **Consistency:** Every guest gets the same quality experience

**Success Metrics:**
- Response time: <30 minutes (business hours), <2 hours (after hours)
- Guest satisfaction: 4.8+ average rating
- Review rate: 70%+ of guests leave reviews
- Repeat booking rate: 15%+ (return guests)
- Issue escalation rate: <5% (most problems solved by VA)

## Section 1: Communication Channels & Tools

### **Primary Channel: PMS Unified Inbox**

**Why:** All OTA messages (Airbnb, Vrbo, Booking.com) flow into one inbox in Hostaway/Guesty

**Setup:**
- [ ] VA has login access to PMS
- [ ] Mobile app installed for after-hours monitoring
- [ ] Push notifications enabled for urgent messages
- [ ] Message tagging system configured (urgent, maintenance, inquiry, etc.)

**Workflow:**
1. Message arrives in unified inbox
2. VA sees notification within 5 minutes
3. VA reads message and categorizes (routine vs. urgent)
4. VA responds or escalates based on type (see below)
5. VA marks message as resolved or flags for follow-up

---

### **Secondary Channels (Rare but Possible)**

**Direct Email:**
- Guests sometimes email directly if they have your contact
- Redirect to PMS: "Please message us through the Airbnb/Vrbo app so we can assist you faster"

**Phone Calls:**
- Only for true emergencies (can't get in, flood, fire, injury)
- Emergency number provided in check-in instructions
- VA or Agency Manager answers, logs issue in PMS

**SMS/Text:**
- Some PMS systems support SMS
- Same response protocols as PMS messages

## Section 3: Tone & Voice Guidelines

### **Brand Voice: "Boutique Hotel Meets Friendly Neighbor"**

**Do:**
- ‚úÖ Use guest's first name
- ‚úÖ Be warm but professional
- ‚úÖ Use clear, simple language
- ‚úÖ Empathize with issues ("I understand how frustrating that must be")
- ‚úÖ Over-communicate during problems
- ‚úÖ End with your name (builds personal connection)

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Use slang or overly casual language ("yo," "hey buddy")

## Section 4: Emergency Protocols

### **True Emergencies (Immediate Escalation to Agency Manager)**

**Definition:** Situations requiring immediate human intervention, not just messaging

**Types:**
1. **Guest Safety:** Injury, medical emergency, fire, flood, break-in, assault
2. **Property Safety:** Flood, fire, gas leak, structural damage
3. **Legal Issues:** Police involvement, domestic dispute, eviction threat
4. **Severe Access Issues:** Can't get in after multiple attempts, stranded outside in extreme weather

**VA Protocol:**
1. Immediately text/call Agency Manager (even after hours)
2. Respond to guest: "I'm escalating this to my manager right now. They'll call you within 5 minutes at [guest phone]."
3. Document everything in PMS with "EMERGENCY" tag
4. Do NOT attempt to resolve alone

---

### **Agency Manager Emergency Response:**

**Step 1: Assess (within 5 minutes)**
- Call guest directly to assess situation
- Determine severity: Does this need 911? Local emergency contact? Or just fast coordination?

**Step 2: Act (within 15 minutes)**
- **If 911 needed:** Instruct guest to call, then you call to report as property manager
- **If local help needed:** Dispatch plumber/locksmith/handyman with "emergency" request
- **If relocation needed:** Offer hotel, arrange booking immediately

**Step 3: Document**
- Log all actions in PMS
- Take photos/screenshots of messages
- Prepare incident report for insurance claim if needed

**Step 4: Follow-Up**
- Check in every 30-60 minutes until resolved
- Proactive compensation (refund/discount)
- Document resolution for future prevention
