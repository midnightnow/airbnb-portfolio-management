# SOP-003: Owner Reporting & Communication
## Monthly Performance Reports and Client Relationship Management

**Version:** 1.0  
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-11  
**Owner:** Agency Manager  
**Purpose:** Standardized process for reporting portfolio performance to owners and maintaining excellent client relationships

---

## Overview

Owner reporting is your retention tool. Transparent, professional, data-rich monthly reports demonstrate value and build trust. Combined with regular communication, these reports ensure clients remain happy and refer new business.

**Core Objectives:**
1. **Transparency:** Owners see every dollar in, every dollar out
2. **Performance Insight:** Data-driven analysis of trends and opportunities
3. **Proactive Communication:** Address issues before owners notice them
4. **Retention:** Happy owners stay for years and expand portfolios
5. **Referrals:** Exceptional reporting creates enthusiastic advocates

**Success Metrics:**
- Owner NPS (Net Promoter Score): 50+
- Client retention rate: 90%+ annually
- Referral rate: 20%+ of clients refer new business
- On-time report delivery: 100% by 5th of month
- Owner satisfaction survey: 8+/10

---

## Section 1: Monthly Reporting Timeline

### **Monthly Cycle:**

**Day 1-3 (First 3 Days of Month):**
- [ ] Export previous month's data from PMS
- [ ] Reconcile revenue and expenses
- [ ] Identify any discrepancies or issues
- [ ] Draft monthly report for each client

**Day 4 (Report Review):**
- [ ] Agency Manager reviews all reports
- [ ] Flags any concerning trends (revenue down, occupancy drop, etc.)
- [ ] Prepares talking points for owner calls
- [ ] Finalizes reports for distribution

**Day 5 (Report Distribution):**
- [ ] Send monthly reports to all owners via email
- [ ] Include PDF + option to view live dashboard in PMS
- [ ] Highlight key metrics and any action items
- [ ] Schedule monthly check-in call (if not already scheduled)

**Day 5-10 (Owner Calls):**
- [ ] 30-minute monthly check-in with each owner
- [ ] Review report together
- [ ] Answer questions
- [ ] Discuss upcoming opportunities or concerns
- [ ] Document action items

**Day 15 (Owner Payouts):**
- [ ] Calculate net owner payout (revenue - expenses - management fee)
- [ ] Process payment via ACH/wire transfer
- [ ] Send payout confirmation email with breakdown
- [ ] Update financial tracking spreadsheet

## Section 4: Financial Reconciliation Process

### **Monthly Reconciliation Checklist**

**Day 1-2 of Month:**

**Step 1: Export Data from PMS**
- [ ] Export all bookings for previous month (CSV or PDF)
- [ ] Export all expenses (cleaning, maintenance, supplies)
- [ ] Export payout schedule (what platforms paid you)
- [ ] Verify all data is complete (no missing bookings)

**Step 2: Reconcile Platform Payments**
- [ ] Check Airbnb payouts received
- [ ] Check Vrbo payouts received
- [ ] Check Booking.com payouts received (if applicable)
- [ ] Compare total received vs. total booking revenue in PMS
- [ ] Flag any discrepancies (e.g., Airbnb held payout due to issue)

**Step 3: Categorize Expenses**
- [ ] Review all expenses in PMS
- [ ] Ensure each expense is assigned to correct property
- [ ] Verify receipts for major expenses (>$100)
- [ ] Flag any unexpected or unusually high expenses

**Step 4: Calculate Management Fee**
- [ ] Management fee = 18% of gross revenue
- [ ] Verify calculation against PMS auto-calculation
- [ ] Ensure fee is clearly documented in owner report

**Step 5: Calculate Net Owner Payout**
```
Gross Revenue (all bookings)
- Operating Expenses (cleaning, maintenance, supplies)
- Management Fee (18%)
= Net Owner Payout
```

**Step 6: Quality Check**
- [ ] Does net payout match expectations? (If way off, investigate)
- [ ] Are there any surprises the owner should know about?
- [ ] Is all data accurately reflected in the monthly report?

## Section 6: Proactive Communication Best Practices

### **When to Reach Out BEFORE the Monthly Report**

**Trigger 1: Significant Revenue Drop**
- If month-to-date revenue is tracking >15% below projection by mid-month
- Proactive email: "Wanted to give you a heads-up that January is running a bit slow due to [reason]. Here's what we're doing..."

**Trigger 2: Major Maintenance Issue**
- Any repair >$500
- Immediate text/call: "FYI, the water heater at Property A needs replacement ($1,200). I'm getting 3 quotes and will have it done this week. Wanted to keep you in the loop."

**Trigger 3: Bad Guest Experience**
- Any incident that could result in bad review or complaint
- Same-day email: "Quick update: Guest at Property B reported [issue]. I handled it within 2 hours and offered a discount. Situation is resolved, but wanted you to know."

**Trigger 4: Excellent Performance**
- Record revenue month, amazing review, or milestone
- Email: "Great news! Property A just had its best month ever ($X,XXX revenue). Your portfolio is crushing it!"

**Why Proactive Communication Matters:**
- âœ… Builds trust (owner never feels surprised)
- âœ… Prevents concerns from festering
- âœ… Shows you're actively managing, not just reporting
- âœ… Strengthens relationship and retention
